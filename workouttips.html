When it comes to losing weight nutrition is the key. If you are overweight, you will definitely look better after losing weight, but if you have just a little bit of fat, losing weight won't improve your physique. What you really need is to build some muscle and shape your body!

Unfortunately only dieting won't help you achieve that. To build muscle you have to start working out, there isn't any shortcut.

BEGINNER

For beginners, it's so simple. All they got to do is start working out. There isn't anything to stress about. Just show up and do the workout. Three times a week full body workout is enough. Do some basic exercise to hit every muscle. It's better to focus more on compound exercise. One of the most important things is to do every exercise with the correct form and full range of motion. Ignore the weight you are using, it doesn't matter whether you are using 5kg more or less. The progress will be visible in a month, even week to week you will start seeing changes on your body.

INTERMEDIATE

As an intermediate, you should start taking track of your workouts. 3-5 times a week is the number of workouts to aim for. Full body split will still work, but now you can change to upper/lower split or push/pull/legs, but I will recommend you to stick to upper/lower. What you need to focus on is Progressive Overload. Progressive overload means increasing your training volume week to week (volume = number of sets x number of reps x weight). But also be careful not to overdo it. You should increase only 10-15% weekly. (For example if you did 3x10x50kg on a given exercise next week do 3x12x50kg or 3x10x55kg). Muscle mind connection is very important, when you are doing an exercise you need to feel the muscle you are hitting. A bad muscle mind connection can be a sign of incorrect way of doing the exercise. Unfortunately when you are an intermediate lifter, your progress will slow down, but of course there will be huge progress in long time periods.

ADVANCED

When you get to the advanced level, that's when things get complicated. Your progress will be really slow due to the fact that you are getting close to your genetic muscle building limit. There are small details in your training that you need to focus on, such as the rep range, rest time in between sets, the order of exercise in the workout, etc. When you've entered this stage you probably have a lot of knowledge and experience about working out and especially your personal needs. You need to have a personalized working plan, good understanding of the volume and type of exercises your body needs.

The only bad workout is the one that you haven't done!
